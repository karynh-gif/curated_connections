Daily Hope
A single line every morning. Something quiet and true. It appears at the top of your space and changes daily.

Circles
Private spaces for
real relationships.
A circle is a group of people who share reflections, play games, and build a shared history together. You control who's in it.
Creating a circle
1
Tap + New Circle
Name it, pick a type — Family, Friends, Couple, Faith, Team, or Custom — and give it a vibe line if you want.
2
Invite your people
Share a link via text, email, or copy it anywhere. Anyone with the link can join. They don't need an account to start.
3
Start sharing
Save any card, reflection, or quiz result and choose to "Offer to a circle." It appears in their circle feed.
What you can share to a circle
📓
Reflections & journal moments
A saved card answer, a Monday Intention, a quiz result — anything from your reflection space.
🎮
Game history
Games played together are recorded in your circle. Scores, winners, and personality lines are all saved.
Sharing is always a choice. Nothing posts to a circle automatically — ever.
Really Bad Day™
Rank. Predict. Survive.
One Judge. Five scenarios. Everyone else tries to think like the Judge. Pass the phone. Reveal everything.
How to play — 5 steps
1
The Judge ranks privately
Five bad scenarios appear. The Judge ranks them from least bad (1) to worst (5) — without showing anyone.
2
Pass the phone
Hand it to the next player. They see the same scenarios — but not the Judge's ranking. They predict how the Judge ranked them.
3
Everyone predicts
Each player does the same. You're not ranking how bad YOU think they are — you're trying to think like the Judge.
4
Score by match
Each correct token scores its number in points. Token 4 correct = 4 points. The Judge scores equal to whoever scored highest.
5
Spin the wheel
The wheel modifies scores each round. Then the Judge passes left and the deck rotates.
🎡 Wheel outcomes
😌

🎁

👑

😈

Six decks rotate each round: Really Bad Day, Survive This, Best Day Ever, Most Likely To, That's My Life, Would You Rather.
That Tracks™
Play the card that fits best.
One prompt. Seven cards in your hand. The Card Holder picks the best match — without knowing who played what.
How to play — 4 steps
1
Card Holder draws a prompt
A green prompt card describes a type of person or situation. The Card Holder sits out this round — they pick the winner.
2
Everyone plays a hand card
You have 7 cards. Pick the one that best fits the prompt. Write-in cards let you write anything — use them once per game.
3
Anonymous reveal
All submissions are read aloud without names. The Card Holder picks the best match.
4
Winner becomes Card Holder
The winner takes the prompt card and becomes the next Card Holder. Most cards at the end wins.
🃏 Plot Twists
🔄
Flip It
Play the opposite — double points if chosen
✍️
Write It
Skip your hand — write anything you want
👥
People's Choice
Everyone votes, not just the Card Holder
🙈
Blind Pick
Card Holder picks without reading cards aloud
😤
Steal It
Argue your card was better after the pick
🎭
Hot Seat
Names revealed before the Card Holder picks
Living Legacy
Preserve the people
you love.
A Living Legacy is a private archive for one person's story — built by the people who knew and loved them. Not after they're gone. While you still have them.
How it works
1
Create a leaf
Start a Living Legacy for someone you love — a parent, grandparent, anyone whose story matters. Write a short note about why you're creating it.
2
Invite contributors
Share a link with family and friends. No account needed. Anyone who receives it can add a memory, photo, voice recording, or video clip.
3
The archive grows
Contributions are organized by life chapter — Childhood, Family, Traditions, Recipes, Lessons, Things Never Said. The archive is permanent.
What people can add
Written stories & memories
In their own words, any length. A smell, a sound, a moment. No writing experience required.
Photos & keepsakes
Recipe cards in her handwriting. Old photos. Letters. Anything that can be photographed.
Their voice & videos
A voicemail. A home movie. An answering machine recording. Their actual voice, laugh, and words — preserved forever.
People forget voices frighteningly fast. This is where you preserve them.
